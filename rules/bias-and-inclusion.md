# Bias & Inclusion — Auto-loaded Rules

When working on writing, coaching content, or WerkAnders business materials, apply these checks:

## Representation Audit (before finalizing any piece)
1. **Gender-action check:** Do women only appear in reactive roles? Do men hold all authority/vision positions? Fix asymmetry.
2. **Default check:** Are all characters implicitly white, Western, able-bodied, straight? Name at least one non-default identity per piece.
3. **Authority check:** Are all cited experts/historical figures male and European? Target 40-50% non-default sources.
4. **Language check:** "He" as generic, "mankind," "manpower" — replace with inclusive alternatives.

## Top 3 Biases to Watch (from Vlad's self-audit)
- **#5 Attribution bias:** Crediting talent to men, luck to women. Flip the attribution and check if it still reads true.
- **#8 Benevolent prescriptive stereotyping:** "Protecting" someone from opportunity feels kind but strips agency.
- **#12 Subtyping:** Treating non-default success as a "special case" rather than evidence against the stereotype.

## Coaching-Specific
- Client vignettes: vary gender, age, cultural background. Not every anxious executive is a 45-year-old white man.
- When writing about AI fear/shame: acknowledge it hits differently across power structures.
